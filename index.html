About Me

I'm currently working on a PhD in applied mathematics at the University of Grenoble, France. Although officially part of the University of Grenoble, France; my working is done at the Complex Systems Institute at the École Normale Supérieure in Lyon, France.

My work orbits around biological (amino acids) structures modeling through network theory and discrete mathemicatics and their applications to biology. My advisors are Claire Lesieur and Laurent Vuillon.

Research Interests

Im currently working and/or interested on the following subjects:

  • Spatial algorithms in 3D
  • Data mining

Code

Currently I'm working on the developpement of a Python module dealing with amino acid networks constructed from structural protein files (e.g. pdb) based on the networkx and biopython packages. Additionally, it contains implementations of algorithms measuring the void or emptyness around amino acids in the structure. The code can be found here.
